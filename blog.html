A little bit of commentary about my travels

I live in New York, but try and get to as many cities as possible in my free time.

While I have been to other places along my travels with highlights

such as weekend trips to Atlantic City.

I have decided to focus on New York, Chicago and Philadelphia I'm just here to share what I enjoyed about each city.

New York


What to do

Get a Citi bike

This is the best way to see the city, I found I got see New York in ways

not available in a taxi on the subway or a tour bus.

Citi Bikes are located on most street corners allowing you to pick

up a bike at Central Park and go all the way to Brooklyn,

taking in the sights of Times Square,Wall Street,The World Trade Centre

South Street Southport and Brooklyn Bridge, at your own pace

Visit Times Square

Times Square is a crazy place especially if it your first time

With most shops open until the late hours it lives up to the title of

the city that never sleeps, it is completely different at night and

less crowded, It's a must see for all regardless of the time but it

really comes to live at night, as the crowds are sparse but the lights

are bright and play tricks on the mind as to what time it is.

City Pass

A City Pass is a must,this allows you to select the places you would

like to visit. You can chose from a list of activities and combine as

many or as few as you wish at a reduced price this way you see the

New York you want to see and can avoid things you have no interest

or time for, I personally bought my pass as you save more and it is

easier to see your options.

Central Park

One of the oldest,most visited, photographed and loved places

in New York. Central Park offers locals and tourists to escape

the madness of NY and relax. With it's endless number of playing

fields you will see everything from soccer to baseball to volleyball.

I recommend getting a bike as the park is huge and you reach the zoo

and other attractions within the park easily.

Sports

Basketball

I went to two basketball games in Brooklyn.One regular one playoff

game.Naturally the playoff was more expensive, however Tickets here

are half the price of the tickets for Madison Square Garden

And the view is definitely better from the barclays centre in Brooklyn

Soccer

Like the football teams the soccer team actually plays there

games in Jersey. I went to a Red Bulls game not expecting much

I was pleasantly surprised, firstly for a front row seat was only 30$

The atmosphere was great and the game was also of a high standard

Baseball

I would encourage everyone to visit Yankee stadium.It an amazing

stadium and the most expensive baseball stadium in the world and

second overall.On game day it is easy to get here and impossible

to leave so don't rush to catch your train or you will be stuck

in a long queue twice as long if you don't have a ticket.Even if

baseball is not your sport go to a game and just enjoy the day

Football

As a Chicago Bears fan it seemed wrong going to see another team play

It was only the Jets tho and who doesn't want to see a team known for

making the most ridiculous mistakes during a game, and it was against

the Eagles who at least have some quality, did I mention I got a free

jersey.The seats were in the heavens but that is all you need if you

go to see the Jets. Or else watch them on TV

Golf

I went to the Barclays, the first playoff event. Played in Liberty State

park which is directly across from the Statue of Liberty and lower

Manhattan. The views were amazing and the course is great too

considering it is built on an old landfill site. Being a golfer

I enjoyed watching the worlds best play with Manhattan serving as the backdrop.

My Favourite

Highline Park

The Highline is one of the coolest places you can visit in New York.

It is an old railroad that has been turned in to a modern park with the emphasis on modern art and history of the railroad.

The park carves it's way through lower manhattan

and offers some great views of he city and empire state.

You can can chill here and forget your in the city.

Bryant Park

This is an open park in the middle of Manhattan just a few streets from Times Square and 5th avenue, the park is used by everyone from

tourists to businessmen on their lunch you will see all types in the park.

Catch a movie here during the summer evenings with the open air cinema or practice your putting at the putting greens

Or play a local in a game of table tennis

99c Pizza

You simply have to get 99c pizza slices at least once during your stay

A 99c Pizza slice is easily the most the diverse crowd of people you

are likely to see anywhere in New York, and you will only find it here,

I had this pizza everyday while I don't encourage that, I do encourage

everyone to have it at least once or twice.

Tenement Museum

This is one the more interesting yet novelty things you can do

America is the land of immigrants with most proud of their roots

Wether you are Irish, Italian, Polish or German this museum is a great

insight in to how families survived and created new lives in what we

would consider un-liveable conditions.

Chicago

Why Chicago

Chicago is my favourite city. There are several reasons why I love this place

These reasons include friendly people, the skyline, the lake and of course the pizza, not to mention the fact it is more relaxed than New York

During the summer you can relax at the lake or shop downtown, or even do both as the lake is beside downtown

Chicago is a great place for everyone whether you are here to see one the sport teams, the bridges, the buildings, shopping or for food.

What to do

The Bean

The cloud gate or better known as the Bean. The Bean is one of the

biggest attractions in Chicago, Located in Millennium Park it is

a must see for everyone. During the summer it can be hard to get a

great photo because of the amount of people at the bean. If it is

quiet when you visit you can have some fun with the different images

that the bean shape makes especially when lying under it.

When visiting the Bean, I prefer to go at night because the

reflection of the skyline at night is cool with the different lights.

Going to the bean during the day there will obviously be more people

however one must the bean during the day and at night.

Sears Tower & Hancock Observatory

The two tallest buildings in Chicago are both open to the public.

While there is no real difference in the views from the two buildings.

On a clear day you can see several different states and great views of

the lake and the city, because of the height only do it on a clear day.

Sears Tower offers the glass floors which are both unique and terrifying

if you are afraid of heights, or paranoid the glass cannot hold everyone

The Hancock Observatory on the other hand is usually a lot less crowded

and as a result you can freely move around,taking in the sights

because there is no time limit at the top you can relax at the cafe and

even order food then sit back and watch Chicago go by below you..

Navy Pier

Navy Pier is Chicago's largest tourist attraction open all year around

During the summer you can come here to watch free firework shows.

Other attractions include Boat trips around the lake, go up the

ferris wheel,Other rides include a musical carousel, the Light

Tower Ride, and Wave Swinger an old-fashioned swinging ride other

Pier Park amusements include an 18-hole miniature golf course and

remote-control boats.Navy Pier has everything for every type of person

fast food, restaurants, bars, shops, mini golf and a large ferris wheel

that offers great views of Chicago.

Shedd Aquarium

Chicago's aquarium is a must see, near to soldier field and the field

museum. I really enjoyed the whole experience. If you do attend the

aquarium, a full pass is a must, This pass will give you access to

all the shows including the Beluga whale and Dolphin shows. This

pass also allows you go the cinema to see a screening of your choice.

You need at least half a day to be able to see all the different

animals, the shark tank is pretty cool as the sharks swim around you

giving a whole panoramic feeling.You can also pet some of the animals

such as the sting rays and different fish.

Sports

American Football

The Chicago Bears are my favourite team, I have been fortunate to

meet most of the team so I always try and convert people to the Bears.

Soldier Field is where the Bears play and you can take a tour of the

stadium if you are visiting during the off-season or just at a time

when there is no game,if there is a game you have to go if you can

get tickets and even if you don't know what is going on you are

guaranteed to enjoy it

Basketball

The Chicago Bulls play their games in the United Centre, You can see

this stadium from the Hancock and Sears tower.Known as the Madhouse

and famous for the Michael Jordan statue outside the stadium, it houses

80 Bulls games a year with games every few days.This makes getting

tickets easier than some other sports and as long as you are visiting

during the season you have a good chance of being able to see a game.

tickets are reasonably priced during the season too

Hockey

The Blackhawks, The Stanley cup winners and Chicago's most

successful team to come out of Chicago in the last number of

years.I am new to Hockey but having followed it religiously

last year I now understand why is a religion in many places

across America.Ridiculously fast and full of hard hits,great

goals and fights Hockey is a sport I would never say

no to attending.

Baseball

Whether you want to go a Cubs or a White Sox game is entirely

your choice, I am not getting involved in the local debates right

now both teams suck anyway, so you might as well just choose the

team that is playing a home game to a team you may actually want

to see if you have no idea and don't care, then I recommend

Wrigley Field solely on the history of the stadium.

Philadelphia

Why Philadelphia

Philadelphia or the city of brotherly love is the second largest city on the east coast

I visited Philadelphia for a historical and cultural trip, I learnt more about early American history here on the tours then I did from textbooks

The best thing about Philadelphia is the fact that the majority of historical and cultural attractions are actually free and run everyday.

And of course Philadelphia has great food.

What to do

Liberty Bell

The Liberty Bell is an iconic symbol of American independence

It originally cracked when first rung after it's arrival in Philadelphia

It is believed it was rang at the time of the declaration of independence

It is now kept in Independence Hall and is free for everybody to see

getting this close to a piece of American history is cool and the tour

that goes with the bell and the gives you the background and history

to allow you appreciate how powerful a symbol the bell was at the time

Rocky Steps

There are 72 steps before the entrance of the Philadelphia Museum of

art or so i was told,These have become known as the Rocky Steps and

there are countless people imitating the movie scenes we did the

customary step run and I was surprised I actually did it, the view

from the top is amazing with a perfect view of the Philadelphia

skyline and plenty of steps to enjoy it from,I never actually got

in to the museum unfortunately

CheeseSteak

The Cheesesteak is an iconic Philadelphia attraction with some vendors

using them as tourist traps charging crazy prices for less authentic

cheesesteaks, on the Philadelphia website there is a list of places

to go and find an authentic vendor and a real cheesesteak and

remember that a cheesesteak in Philadelphia is nothing like a

cheesesteak in a different city.

Reading Terminal Market

Reading Terminal Market is a one stop shop for every choice of

food you could possibly want, from fresh vegatables to sweats

to meat to fish to Amish specialties, I must admit for someone

who has only ever seen the Amish on Tv it was strange seeing them

in real life, however they do make good food.Reading Market is a

great place to catch breakfast or lunch.
