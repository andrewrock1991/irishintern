A little bit about me

I am UI developer living in Dublin. I studied Business information systems in University College Cork, spending my third year on an internship in Manhattan. Having recently graduated I am now living in Dublin. When i'm not working you will find me doing one of three things, supporting the Chicago bears, Manchester United or Bohemian FC. I am extremely passionate about web design and in particular mobile design.

Some of the things I do

Responsive Design

Nothing frustrates me more than when I visit my favourite sites and they are not designed with the mobile in mind. We've all been there when you have to zoom just to read the text. Most of my time is spent browsing the web on my phone. As a result I am determined to ensure my websites will work regardless of the device.

Web Development

I have taught web developemnt to post graduate students in Univeristy College Cork and was fortunate enough to win a developer ticket to the Web Summit in Dublin in my final year of college. I am comfortable working with frontend technologies, whether it is for personal projects, helping friends out or woking on larger scale commercial projects.

Creative Design

I try to spend as much time as possible on creative sites such as Dribbble and Behance. I am always looking for new ideas and fresh approaches to my work. Everytime I visit these sites I leave with tons of new ideas from the extremely talented work people post. I try to incorporate some of these ideas in my work

  • Some of my work

    In my final year I designed a voting application for University elections.

    The goal here was to produce an application that would change the way students both run in elections and how they vote.

    The application allowed students to create an account from which they could either run in an election or vote for their friends.

  • Bootstrap or custom

    Most of work is done through the use of bootstrap as it provides more than enough out of the box.

    For times when a more custom feel is required I am just as comfortable abandoning the grids to create something a little more .
